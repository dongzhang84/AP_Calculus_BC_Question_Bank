The number of students in a school who have heard a rumor at time \(t\) hours is modeled by the function \(P\), the solution to a logistic differential equation. At noon, 50 of the school's 500 students have heard the rumor. Also at noon, \(P\) is increasing at a rate of 20 students per hour. Which of the following could be the logistic differential equation?

(A) \(\frac{dP}{dt} = \frac{1}{1125} P(500 - P)\)
(B) \(\frac{dP}{dt} = \frac{1}{480} P(500 - P)\)
(C) \(\frac{dP}{dt} = \frac{1}{192} P(500 - P)\)
(D) \(\frac{dP}{dt} = \frac{2}{45} P(500 - P)\)
(E) \(\frac{dP}{dt} = \frac{5}{48} P(500 - P)\)
