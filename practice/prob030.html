A function \(f\) has a Maclaurin series given by \(2 + 3x + x^2 + \frac{1}{3}x^3 + \cdots\), and the Maclaurin series converges to \(f(x)\) for all real numbers \(x\). If \(g\) is the function defined by \(g(x) = e^{f(x)}\), what is the coefficient of \(x^2\) in the Maclaurin series for \(g\)?

(A) \(\frac{1}{2} e^2\)
(B) \(e^2\)
(C) \(\frac{5}{2} e^2\)
(D) \(\frac{11}{2} e^2\)
