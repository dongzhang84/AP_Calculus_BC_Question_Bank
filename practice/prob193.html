A population of wolves is modeled by the function \( P \) and grows according to the logistic differential equation \[ \frac{dP}{dt} = 5P\left(1 - \frac{P}{5000}\right), \] where \( t \) is the time in years and \( P(0) = 1000 \). Which of the following statements are true?

  1. \(\lim_{{t \to \infty}} P(t) = 5000\)
  2. \(\frac{dP}{dt}\) is positive for \(t > 0\).
  3. \(\frac{d^2P}{dt^2}\) is positive for \(t > 0\).
(A) I only
(B) II only
(C) I and II only
(D) I and III only
(E) I, II, and III
