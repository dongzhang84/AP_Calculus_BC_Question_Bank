A cup has the shape of a right circular cone. The height of the cup is 12 cm, and the radius of the opening is 3 cm. Water is poured into the cup at a constant rate of 2 cm\(^3\)/sec. What is the rate at which the water level is rising when the depth of the water in the cup is 5 cm? (The volume of a cone of height \(h\) and radius \(r\) is given by \( V = \frac{1}{3} \pi r^2 h \).)

(A) \(\frac{32}{25\pi}\) cm/sec
(B) \(\frac{96}{125\pi}\) cm/sec
(C) \(\frac{2}{3\pi}\) cm/sec
(D) \(\frac{2}{9\pi}\) cm/sec
(E) \(\frac{1}{200\pi}\) cm/sec
